USC

  • Distributed Neural Network Trainer: I wrote a program that would construct the infrastructure for a peer to peer network to train large neural networks. Hosts submit payloads (input, output, model specifications) to the network, where the load is distributed amongst other hosts, and the data is aggregated and returned to the original host, who receives a fully trained neural network. This method allows for 400-500% increases in training speed as opposed to training on a single machine.
  • Skynet: ITP342 (iOS development) final project. “Skynet” is a mobile application which will allow you to take a photograph and will use machine learning technology to either merge the image with the style of a famous painting, or classify the contents of the image with corresponding confidence percentage
  • One Percent Project: Web application in which you set a goal for yourself, and then choose people in your life to hold you accountable for daily progress towards your goal. On days where you do not submit a daily progress report, the application will email blast all your friends so they can help get you back on track -- Github
  • Chess Engine: Chess AI that uses Alpha Beta Pruning (Minimax Variant) to generate all possible moves 3-6 steps ahead of a normal human player
  • Custom NCAA Ranking System: Linear Algebra simulation that will rank NCAA college football teams from a purely mathematical/data-oriented perspective. It can account for various tiers of the NCAA structure, and also accounts for teams that play games outside of the NCAA structure altogether
  • Playlist Optimizer: Python script that utilizes the musical characteristic analysis of Spotify and basic linear algebra to find the optimal flow of songs in a playlist based solely off of the songs content. Think of it as a music reccomendation engine that reccomends songs not based on collaborative filtering (what others who are listening to this song also listen to), but on content based filtering (what songs sound the most like this song)
  • Campsite Booker: Browser Automation script to book a campsite in a national park given a search term, desired nights staying at campsite, number of occupants, and a date range. I've personally used this to book last minute campsites in highly trafficked national parks including Yosemite (on Labor Day weekend!) and Mt. Rainier National Park only days in advance, when it is normally reserved months in advance. Open sourcing this full capability would cause it to be abused, so instead, I am planning to add it as a functionality to this website, so that I can incorporate rate limiting based on API keys.
  • Corpus Callosum: USC Organization that encouraged creative combinations of art and technology. I lead a team of software and hardware developers to build a smart plant habitat that would tweet at you when it needed water or sunlight. Wrote core software for arduino.
